The history of tourism and its potential

      Traveling is one of the pleasures for many people regardless of nationality in the tedious days. In the middle ages and early modern, most of the traveling purpose was a pilgrimage, and many people could not visit distant places because traveling cost much money. In modern, thanks to the development of transportation like cars, many people could enjoy traveling, but still, it was not common amusement. Now, traveling has become more popular. The growth rate of the traveling and tourism industry in the world has exceeded that of the economy in the world for nine years (World Travel & Tourism Council, 2019). Especially in Japan, tourism has become one of the vital elements of the economy. Not only Asian people but also European and Americans visit Japan for a trip, and there are many reasons they choose to travel to Japan. To make Japanese tourism more fascinating, the Japanese government should promote the following things: healthy and delicious food, the hot spring, historical sightseeing spots, Kendama, and real-life anime locations.

Food

      One of the things the Japanese government should promote to attract many people to Japan is delicious and healthy food. Compared to Chinese cuisine like Mapo Tofu, which uses various seasonings, most Japanese dishes make the most of the original

taste of local ingredients. Besides, Japanese people have traditionally emphasized the style of Ichiju-Sansai (Figure1), which means one soup and three dishes consisting of a variety of seasonal vegetables. Therefore, Japanese meals are tasty and good for health. For example, sushi is a famous Japanese food in the world. Japan is an insular country surrounded by ocean and has optimum fishing grounds, so Japanese dishes incorporate various fishes. According to the medical center of the Toho University (2011), fish contains docosahexaenoic acid (DHA) and eicosatetraenoic acid (EPA) (Figure2) in comparison with meats . DHA and EPA decrease the amount of bad cholesterol and are helpful to deal with lifestyle diseases like obesity and arteriosclerosis that are spreading in the USA and Europe. About 71 percent of people in the world passed away because of lifestyle diseases triggered by the spread of fast food (World Health Organization, 2018). Therefore, Japanese food can improve people’s health in developed countries. Japanese tourism will be more captivating for those interested in healthy life if the government promotes the greatness of palatable Japanese food.

Hot Spring

      Another thing the Japanese government should propel to increase the number of tourists is the hot spring. Tourists can be exhausted by a long time on the plane or train. Moreover, unfamiliar local climate may fatigue the visitors. However, one of the purposes of traveling is to chill out. Hence, after people enjoy visiting a historical site or shopping and come back to the hotel or Japanese Inn, people can make themselves at home and relieve the weariness of the travel by taking a hot spring bath with beautiful scenery in Japan. Taking a hot spring makes people’s blood flow smoothly and has an effect of detoxification by improving metabolism (Japanese Ministry of the Environment, 2020). Although foreigners who have a tattoo sometimes cannot get into the hot spring because of the traditional Japanese image that people with a tattoo have a connection with anti-social forces, the Japanese government declares that restricting tattoos is not appropriate in response to Tokyo Olympic and Paralympic (Miyano, 2019). Therefore, more and more foreign tourists can enjoy traditional hot springs.

Historical Sightseeing Spot

      Historical sightseeing spot is one of the contents of tourism that the Japanese administration ought to promote. One of the chief purposes of tourism is to experience

and touch local traditional cultures. In Japan, Kyoto is famous for traditional Japanese temples and shrines. There is a limitation of the height and color of architecture in Kyoto by the Kyoto scenery treaty to keep the good old town. By looking at the Preservation district of historic buildings and Priorities area of the plan for the maintenance and improvement of historic scenic beauty (Figure3), Kyoto emphasizes the importance of historical buildings compared to Tokyo. For example, though the color of McDonald's billboards (Figure4) consists of red and yellow, most of them in Kyoto are brown to synchronize the shops with a historical view. Thus, foreigners can fully undergo the traditional modest culture. In addition to old streets and houses, many temples and shrines are worth visiting to understand Japanese history. The Kinkaku-Ji (Gold Temple) (Figure5) is one of the most famous temples in Japan. Yoshimitsu Ashikaga built this temple to show his supreme authority in the Kamakura period, so it is gold and gorgeous temple. On the other hand, the Ginkaku-Ji (Silver Temple) (Figure5) built by Yoshimasa Ashikaga in the Kamakura period is frugal but pioneered the Japanese traditional architectural system. Both temples are World Heritage Site, but the contrast between Kinkaku-Ji and Ginkaku-Ji is intriguing and helpful to understand Japanese culture.

Kendama

      Kendama (Figure6) can be a potential promotion of traveling for Japanese governments. According to the Japan Kendama Association, Kendama is the Japanese

version of the Cup & Ball game, consisting of a handle and a spike with a ball connected by a string. Though the origin of the Kendama is off public debate, many think that it started in France as Bilboquet in the 16 century. After that, Kendama settled down in Japan and formed one of the traditional Japanese cultures. Recently, thanks to Colin Sander, founder of KendamaUSA, Kendama has been gradually raising its recognition in the USA. Whereas tricks of Kendama in Japan are often low-key, those developed in the USA are relatively acrobatic. These differences of tricks in other cultures mean that Kendama players can choose the way they fit. For instance, tricks at beginners level like Furiken (Swing In) or Tōdai (Lighthouse) (Figure7) would be appropriate to practice for the elderly since their difficulties lie in concentration. However, tricks in an aerial level like Inazuma Otoshi Ikkaiten Tōdai (Lightning drop 1-turn lighthouse) (Figure8) would be proper for the young since their difficulties lie in speed. In this way, Kendama is a traditional Japanese toy with no limitation of age and gender. Besides, Kendama is easy to play with compared to other major sports like soccer, baseball, and American football as long as players have their Kendama. Under the COVID-19, Kendama has spread online and will enable those who shut themselves in their house to exercise at home. Therefore, Kendama will be one of the plausible ways to allure the foreigners.

Anime Tourism

      The last thing the Japanese government should encourage to draw the attention of foreigners is anime tourism. Japanese anime has become popular because of its high quality and various genres. The sales of the anime industry have increased for six years, approximately half of which were from other countries (Association of Japanese Animations, 2019). Due to the Coronavirus, most people have spent more time at home since April. Therefore, many people can have time to enjoy watching anime. For example, Pocket Monster is one of the most famous Japanese animations in foreign countries. Pokémon GO is also a widespread game, whose players walk to catch rare Pokémon on their smartphone. Therefore, Pokémon GO can be one of the incentives for travelers to visit Japan by setting regionally limited Pokémon for enthusiastic fans. Also, it is one of the new styles of tourism to go to a real-life anime location. By manufacturing anime goods for foreign fans limited to the area, anime pilgrimage will promote the local economy.

Beyond the Barrier of Language

      While there is an enormous potential for Japanese tourism, some foreigners are reluctant to visit Japan because of the barrier of languages. Nevertheless, this argument can be refutable. Firstly, Japanese governments and corporations have introduced a

multilingual display (Figure9)and sound for English, Korean, and Chinese. Transportations, hotels, and tourist spots are the principal place where Japanese people should support multi-languages. More than 95 percent of the homepage of the subway and bus in Japan is compatible with English (Japanese Tourism Agency, 2020). Therefore, travelers can check the appropriate route in English. Plus, most of the main transportations like airplanes and bullet trains are compatible with a foreign language in response to the increase of Japanese tourism. Japanese hotels and tourist sites have also improved multilingual support due to the Tokyo Olympic and Paralympic. Secondly, most Japanese will kindly try to show the way, if spoken, although Japanese people are not good at speaking English. Consequently, travelers can experience the politeness and kindness of Japanese people. Thus, the “barrier of the languages” argument does not stand to think about multilingual support and Japanese tenderness.

Conclusion

      In conclusion, delicious food, hot springs, historical sites, Kendama, and anime pilgrimage are the main reasonable promotions for the Japanese government to attract foreign tourists. The former two are for people whose purposes are to have relaxed while the latter three are for people who want to experience traditional and present Japanese cultures. Japan has also become more compatible with foreign languages. By promoting the above five things, Japan will be more famous for its humble and graceful culture.
