      Two bar graphs show the amount of docosahexaenoic acid (DHA) and eicosatetraenoic acid (EPA). Blue bars indicate the amount of DHA and EPA in fishes while orange bars indicate that in meats including pork, chicken, and beef. It is clear from the two graphs that fishes contain a large amount of both EPA and DHA in comparison with meat.
